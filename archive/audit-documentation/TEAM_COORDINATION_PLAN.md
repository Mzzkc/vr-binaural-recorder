## ESCALATION AND CONFLICT RESOLUTION

### Escalation Triggers

**IMMEDIATE ESCALATION (Stop work, notify all):**
- Recording functionality added to codebase
- Team member unclear on corrected vision
- Client requirements clarification needed
- Major technical blocker identified

**24-HOUR ESCALATION (Review and resolution required):**
- Architecture decision disagreement
- Governance procedure violation
- Resource allocation conflict
- Timeline impact concern

**WEEKLY ESCALATION (Strategic review needed):**
- Process improvement requirement
- Tool enhancement need
- Training requirement identified
- Success metric adjustment needed

### Conflict Resolution Process

**Step 1: Direct Resolution (Same day)**
- Team members discuss issue directly
- Solution Architect provides technical guidance
- Project Manager facilitates if needed

**Step 2: Management Review (24 hours)**
- Project Manager assesses situation
- Solution Architect validates technical aspects
- Coordinated resolution plan developed

**Step 3: Client Consultation (48 hours)**
- Major impacts discussed with client
- Strategic direction clarification
- Resource adjustment approval

## RISK MANAGEMENT AND CONTINGENCY

### Identified Coordination Risks

**High-Risk Scenarios:**
1. **Team Member Confusion:** Unclear understanding of corrected vision
   - Mitigation: Daily vision alignment checks, documentation reviews
   - Contingency: Immediate retraining and one-on-one clarification

2. **Process Resistance:** Team members resist governance procedures
   - Mitigation: Clear rationale explanation, collaborative improvement
   - Contingency: Management escalation and accountability measures

3. **Technical Disagreements:** Architecture decisions conflict
   - Mitigation: Clear decision authority matrix, expert consultation
   - Contingency: Client involvement in strategic decisions

4. **Timeline Pressure:** Rushed decisions compromise governance
   - Mitigation: Protected time for proper review processes
   - Contingency: Scope adjustment negotiation with client

**Medium-Risk Scenarios:**
- Communication breakdown between team members
- Tool or automation failure impacting tracking
- External dependency changes affecting timeline
- Client requirement clarification delays

### Contingency Plans

**Team Coordination Failure:**
- Emergency team meeting within 24 hours
- Individual skill and understanding assessment
- Process simplification if needed
- Additional training or resource allocation

**Technical Execution Problems:**
- Solution Architect emergency review
- Veteran Engineer rapid assessment
- Alternative approach identification
- Client impact communication

**Client Relationship Issues:**
- Immediate Project Manager escalation
- Transparent problem communication
- Solution proposal development
- Timeline and expectation reset
